About AbujaFix

We believe every pothole deserves a public record.

AbujaFix is Abuja's first independent, citizen-powered infrastructure reporting platform. We exist because the people of FCT deserve a city that works — and because silence has never fixed a road.

Our mission

To make every infrastructure failure in Abuja visible, documented and impossible to ignore — until it is fixed.

The problem

Abuja is broken — and nobody is keeping score

The Federal Capital Territory is home to over 3 million residents and receives billions of naira in annual government allocations. Yet its roads are riddled with potholes, its drainage systems overflow every rainy season, its streetlights stand dark for months, and construction projects are abandoned midway — with no accountability whatsoever.

The biggest problem is not just the broken infrastructure. It is the complete absence of any public record. There is no single place where citizens can see what is broken, where it is broken, how long it has been broken, and whether anyone is doing anything about it.

Until now.

Our solution

How AbujaFix works

AbujaFix is built on a simple but powerful idea — that citizens, not government, are the most reliable source of truth about the state of their own city. We give every resident the tools to document what they see and put it on a public map that the entire world can access.

1
Citizens report problems
Any resident opens AbujaFix, drops a pin on the map, describes the problem and submits. No account required. No bureaucracy. The report goes live in seconds.
2
Reports build a public record
Every submission is stored permanently in our database and displayed on a live map. The more reports pile up in one area, the clearer the evidence becomes — and the harder it is for anyone to deny.
3
Data creates accountability
Our map is fully public and accessible to journalists, NGOs, researchers and government officials. When media covers our data, when activists cite our reports, when international organisations reference our findings — government is forced to respond.
4
Problems get fixed
When a reported problem is resolved, it is marked as fixed on the map. This creates a public record of wins — proving that citizen action works and motivating more participation.

Our values

What we stand for

Radical transparency
Every report is public. Every data point is verifiable. We never hide or filter information that citizens have a right to see.
Complete independence
AbujaFix is not owned by government, funded by politicians or controlled by any institution with a conflict of interest. We answer only to the citizens of Abuja.
Citizen first
Every design decision, every feature, every priority is driven by one question — does this make it easier for Abuja residents to be heard?
Verified accuracy
We do not publish data we cannot verify. Every statistic on this platform is sourced from credible Nigerian publications and government records.

Our impact

What we are building toward

AbujaFix is in its early days — but our vision is clear. We are building the most comprehensive, real-time infrastructure database ever created for any Nigerian city. Here is what that looks like in practice:

A complete infrastructure map
Every bad road, broken light and blocked drain in Abuja — documented, geotagged and timestamped.
Monthly data reports
Regular published reports showing infrastructure trends by area — given freely to media, NGOs and government.
Government accountability
A public record that makes it impossible for FCDA and FCT Administration to claim ignorance of infrastructure failures.

Our story

How AbujaFix was born

AbujaFix was founded in 2026 by a young Abuja resident who was tired of complaining about the same potholes, the same broken lights, the same flooded streets — with nothing changing. No tools existed specifically for Abuja residents to document and report these problems. The platforms that existed either focused on government budgets, were controlled by government, or were national tools too broad to be useful at the street level.

"I didn't build AbujaFix to make money. I built it because I believe Abuja deserves better — and because I knew that if citizens had the right tools, they could hold their city accountable in ways that government cannot ignore."

— Founder, AbujaFix

AbujaFix was built from scratch with zero budget — just a laptop, determination and the conviction that technology can be a tool for civic good. Every line of code, every design decision and every piece of verified data on this platform was produced independently, without investors, without grants and without government support.
